Welcome to the Thayer Lab!

The Thayer Lab is a lab at Wesleyan University, in Middletown, Connecticut. Our goal is simple: to cure cancer with computers.

The Thayer Lab has been run by Kelly Thayer since _____. We are at the forefront of science in the field of Molecular Dynamics, an exciting field of science that allows the study of macromolecules, namely proteins and DNA. With this powerful tool at our disposal, we are able to investigate everything from straightforward topics like how the presence of a drug will impact the behavior of a protein to extremely abstract topics, such as how allosteric signals propagate through a protein.

We are specifically interested in the protein p53: the famous Guardian of the Genome. Over 50% of human cancers feature a mutated form of p53. Though we are interested in all mutations of p53, one specific mutation interests us especially: the Y220C mutation, which replaces the cystine amino acid 220 with a tyrosine. We are so interested in this because there the drug PK11000 has been shown eliminate Y220C-p53-induced tumors in vivo, albeit with dire side effects.

Thus our lab is focused on studying how PK11000 is able to restore the wild type function of the Y220C p53 mutation, and how we can use this information to design a smarter and more targeted drug. Once this has been done, we hope to generalize our methods to other mutations and other splice isoforms of p53.
