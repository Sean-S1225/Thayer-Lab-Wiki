RMSF: Root Mean Squared Fluctuation

View the code cookbook for RMSF code snippets.

The Root Mean Squared Fluctuation (RMSF) is a very common type of analysis. It is similar to RMSD in that it reports deviation with reference to some sort of common substructure, but instead of providing a single number for the overall deviation between a protein and a reference structure, it reports the deviation between individual amino acids (or atoms). Typically, when RMSF is calculated over a whole simulation, the average structure is used as reference, and a single number represents an amino acid's deviation over the course of the whole simulation.

Higher values indicate regions where the protein is more mobile over the course of the simulation, and lower values indicate regions where the protein is stable.

The RMSF of amino acid \(i\) is calculated using the following formula:

\[\text{RMSF}_i = \sqrt{\langle(x_i - \langle x_i\rangle)^2\rangle}\]

where \(x_i\) is the \(i\)-th residue of the protein \(x\), and \(\langle x_i\rangle\) is the average position of residue \(i\) over the course of the simulation.

Typically, RMSF restricts analysis to only the alpha-carbons or heavy backbone atoms.

  • Within stable domains, regions such as 179-193 and 220-234 are highly mobile. These are areas of interest for further studies.

As is with RMSD, RMSF is more of a first-pass analysis that can provide valuable insight about where to focus additional attention as the study of a simulation continues.
